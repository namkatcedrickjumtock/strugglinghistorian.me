# Mission

## Why this blog exists

*The Struggling Historian* is a personal blog where Cedrick Jumtock publishes long-form writing on African history, colonial memory, digital humanities, and the intersection of technology and historical methodology.

The name is intentional: history is never clean, complete, or comfortable. This is not a textbook site. It is a place to sit with the mess — to ask questions that don't have satisfying answers, to follow threads that don't tie up neatly.

## The problem it solves

Most history writing online is either academic (locked behind paywalls, written for specialists) or popular-history adjacent (oversimplified, structured for engagement over accuracy). There is a gap for long-form, rigorous, but accessible history writing that:
- Takes sources seriously
- Acknowledges what the archive cannot tell us
- Uses technology (code, diagrams, visualisations) as a legitimate tool of historical inquiry
- Speaks to readers who are curious but not credentialled historians

## Reader personas

**The Curious Non-Historian**
Someone who reads broadly, thinks carefully, and wants more than Wikipedia but isn't going to read a 400-page monograph. They follow ideas across disciplines. They want context, not just facts.

**The Student / Early Researcher**
An undergraduate or postgraduate in history, African studies, or digital humanities. They are looking for methodological examples, not just content — how does someone think through a historical problem?

**The Developer-Historian crossover**
Someone who works in tech but has a parallel interest in history. They are comfortable with code examples and diagrams. They are specifically interested in the digital humanities angle.

## Success metrics

- Posts are published consistently (at least one per month once underway)
- Posts are shared and linked to in academic and public-history circles
- The site is findable via search for specific historical topics
- Cedrick can invite speaking/writing opportunities through the Sessionize profile linked from the site

## What this blog is NOT

- Not a news site or current events commentary blog
- Not an academic journal (citations are used where relevant, not required)
- Not a travel or personal diary blog
- Not a product or service — no monetisation planned in the initial phase
